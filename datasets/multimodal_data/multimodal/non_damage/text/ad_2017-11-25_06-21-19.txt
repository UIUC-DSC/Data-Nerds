No less an expert than Karl Lagerfeld called Carla Bruni a “beautiful creature who can wear anything.” Or nothing, he might have added. By 1991 she could be found in *Vogue*, dominating a group shot of supermodels, each glamazon vamping her heart out in a skintight leopard Alaia catsuit. But Carla alone gazes piercingly out of the picture, “with the look”—to use the words of one love rival—“of a killer.”
